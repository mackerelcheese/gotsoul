Superman Saves A Schoolbus After Bridge Collapse
Three adults and thirty elementry school students were in the bus as the bridge collapsed. Photo: Joey Day/Gotham Gazette.
by Clark Kent

METROPOLIS - Two adults and 24 Gotham Elementary students were involved when the East Gotham-Metropolis bridge collapsed earlier today.

Superman was able to rescue them as well as evacuate other motorists on the road. Three of the students and the driver had minor injuries and were sent to the Metropolis General Hospital.

"The teachers, Ms Janet and Ms Lucia, did an incredible job keeping their students calm during the whole ordeal," Superman stated after incident. "Teachers like them are the shining light of their communities."

The Gotham Elementary students were returning from a field trip to the Metropolis Science Museum.

Bruce Wayne, chairman of the Wayne Foundation has issued a statement that his foundation would be covering any medical and mental health costs for those involved in today's incident.

Police said that they are not ruling out any possible reasons for the bridge failure at the moment and are waiting for investigations to conclude.

At time of publishing, Gotham Elementry and the Gotham Education Board were unavailable for comment.

The Daily Planet Welcomes New CEO Effective Immediately
1 hour ago
Photo by Jimmy Olsen/The Daily Planet
by vicki vale

METROPOLIS - The Daily Planet Company has appointed Marisol Leone as CEO effective immediately.

She will be succeeding Morgan Edge, who will be stepping down due to his sudden appointment as President of the Galaxy Broadcasting System.

"Leone brings her more than 20 year experience in management, financial expertise, and leadership to the role."

"She was the COO of RedWallet, the number one app for cyrptocurrency and online banking, for the past five years. Prior to that, she was the CFO of Galaxy Publishing, the publishing arm of Galaxy Communications," the company stated in a press release.

Leone also owns shares in Cale-Anderson Pharmaceuticals.

Editor's note: Wayne Enterprises, which owns the Gotham Gazette, has a 15.5% interest in The Daily Planet Company.
